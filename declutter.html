PROJECT OVERVIEW

Declutter is a mobile app that (1) allows users to set alarms based on their sleep cycles in a more organized and intuitive manner, (2) generate sleep soundtracks tailored to enhancing sleep quality, and (3) destress, decompress, and declutter at the end of the day by providing a platform for users to journal or create a to-do list.

1. DEFINING THE PROBLEM SPACE

The target audience for this project will focus primarily on university students. The main objectives of this project will be to uncover key frustrations and pain points with regards to students’ sleep experience as well as get an overarching idea of students’ sleep patterns and routines.

2. USER RESEARCH AND USER PERSONAS

User research was conducted on a voluntary basis with students at the University of California, Berkeley. Data from user research was used to generate a current state journey map as well as a user persona to identify key opportunities for app ideation.

Interviews during user research revealed that students experienec the lowest dips in energy in the morning and in the middle of the day as a result of poor quality sleep the night before and having a large number of commitments throughout the day without a clear plan of action. Napping 1-2 times per day is common but a lack of time and energy in the evening for journaling or reflection makes it more difficult to get better sleep.

3. INITIAL CONCEPTS AND MID-FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

7. REFLECTION: THE FUTURE OF DECLUTTER

In making my way through the design process involved with prototyping Declutter, I came to realize how critical usability testing and gaining a understanding a user’s mental models (particularly for apps with various flows) was to ultimately creating a more user centric and intuitive product. In future iterations of Declutter, I hope to iterate on a better way to transition between the “Nap”, “Sleep”, and “Declutter” flows so that the app is more cohesive and consistent.
